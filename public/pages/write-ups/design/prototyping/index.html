Prototyping

What design questions did your intend to answer with your prototype(s)?

Could you tell what you needed to do?

Do the starting mechanisms feel appropriate?

Is the initial story compelling and logical?

Does the medium of the story match with the mechanisms?

How did you design your prototype(s) and subsequent user studies to address these questions?

We made our prototype a mock tutorial of our game, with the main production centered around papyrus –> paper instead of wood –> paper. This allowed us to mock up the start of the game, as the introduction of idle mechanics was something we wanted to focus on. We had little paper signs and scripted instructions to guide players. We also gave players a small story summary before playing so they would have an idea of the greater context.

What answers to your design questions did your efforts unveil?

Having a tutorial may create a story/gameplay mismatch, as give players background and then essentially make them do the same introductory idle process twice. Adding sound effects and dynamic UI could help keep people engaged. Another engagement boost is accomodating different playstyles (min/maxing, exploring, casual, etc). Finally, the production mechanics seemed to match well with the story and vibe we were trying to convey.
