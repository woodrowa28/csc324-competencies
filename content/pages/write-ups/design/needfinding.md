# Needfinding


What techniques did you employ to discover users' needs?

> As a group, we conducted multiple interviews and observations of our peers to learn how they tend to interact with idle games. Personally, I conducted one open-ended interview and one idle game observation. In the interview, I let the person talk freely about their view on idle games and prompted with more questions as needed; in the observation, I had the participant play an idle game they had never played before and narrate their thought process and actions as they played.

What did you learn about users' needs from this process?

> Our interviews made it very obvious that idle games appeal to a very specific and narrow group of people. If we want to draw more people in, we should combine elements, such as idle and RPG, to ensure that more people have a reason to play. This could involve some sort of storyline/dialogue, a tangible and satisfying ending, and some visual feedback.
